A Guide to Boosting Your Appetite: Tips and Supplements You Need to Know

Published on 24 April 2023

In General:

Appetite refers to the desire to eat, and it is an important bodily function that helps us maintain our health by providing the necessary nutrients and energy needed for our bodies to function properly.

How To Increase Your Appetite:

  1. Eat smaller, more frequent meals: Rather than trying to force down large meals, try eating smaller meals more frequently throughout the day. This can help to stimulate the appetite and prevent feelings of fullness.
  2. Choose nutrient-dense foods: Eating foods that are high in nutrients, such as fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains, can help to increase appetite and promote overall health.
  3. Add healthy fats: Including healthy fats in the diet, such as avocado, nuts, and olive oil, can help to increase the feeling of fullness and stimulate the appetite.
  4. Engage in physical activity: Exercise can help to stimulate the appetite by increasing the body's metabolism and creating a greater need for energy.
  5. Manage stress: Stress can negatively impact the appetite, so it is important to find ways to manage stress, such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.
  6. Drink fluids before and after meals: Drinking fluids before and after meals can help to stimulate the appetite and make it easier to consume more food. However, it is important to avoid drinking too much fluid during meals, as this can make you feel too full and decrease your appetite.

Supplements To Increase Appetite:

  1. Omega-3 fatty acids: Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats that are found in fish, nuts, and seeds. Some research suggests that omega-3 supplements may help to increase appetite, particularly in people who are experiencing weight loss or have a low appetite due to a medical condition.
  2. Probiotics: Probiotics are live bacteria and yeasts that are found in certain foods and supplements. Some studies have suggested that probiotics may help to increase appetite and improve digestion.
  3. Vitamin B12: Vitamin B12 is a nutrient that is important for many aspects of health, including the production of red blood cells and the functioning of the nervous system. Some research has suggested that vitamin B12 supplements may help to increase appetite, particularly in older adults who may be at risk of vitamin B12 deficiency.
  4. Ashwagandha: Ashwagandha is an herb that is commonly used in Ayurvedic medicine. Some research has suggested that ashwagandha supplements may help to increase appetite and improve weight gain, particularly in people who are experiencing stress-related loss of appetite.

Importance Of B Vitamin Complex:

B complex vitamins are a group of water-soluble vitamins that play important roles in many aspects of health. The B vitamins include thiamin (B1), riboflavin (B2), niacin (B3), pantothenic acid (B5), pyridoxine (B6), biotin (B7), folate (B9), and cobalamin (B12). Each B vitamin has a specific function in the body. For example, thiamin helps to convert food into energy, riboflavin is important for maintaining healthy skin and eyes, and niacin plays a role in the metabolism of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins. The B vitamins are found naturally in many foods, including whole grains, fruits, vegetables, meats, and dairy products. However, some people may have difficulty getting enough B vitamins from their diet alone. For example, people who follow a vegetarian or vegan diet may be at risk of vitamin B12 deficiency, as this vitamin is primarily found in animal-based foods. In addition to their individual roles in the body, the B vitamins also work together to support overall health. For example, vitamin B6 is needed for the body to make neurotransmitters, which are important for regulating mood, while vitamin B12 is necessary for the production of red blood cells. Supplements containing B complex vitamins are available and may be recommended in cases of deficiency or for people who may have increased nutrient needs, such as pregnant women or people with certain medical conditions.
